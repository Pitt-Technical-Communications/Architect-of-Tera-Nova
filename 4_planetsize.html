To correctly select your planet's size, you must understand the following: all objects with mass exert gravitational pull, and a planet's atmosphere is constantly trying to escape this pull. If a planet is too small, it lacks the gravity needed to retain its atmosphere.

According to a Harvard study, a planet must be at least 2.7% of Earth’s mass (approximately 1.61 × 10²³ kilograms) to hold its atmosphere, as shown in the illustration below.
